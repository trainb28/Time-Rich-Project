The reality

Corporate ends for every one of us.

Some will hang on longer than others, but it ends. That is not a scare tactic, it is arithmetic. Careers are ending earlier, pensions are gone, and we are living decades longer than the old model was built for.

If you are still in it, you may not see it coming. I did not. They made the decision for me. What I decided was that I was not going back.

Nothing about this is easy. But if everything is hard, choose the hard that benefits you.
Hang on

Keep your head down, hope it lasts, and figure it out when the decision gets made for you. Most people pick this one by default, not on purpose.

Build

Start now, while you still have runway. Decide what you want, work out how to fund it, and meet the exit on your terms instead of theirs.

What to expect

The unknown is the scary part.

Most people picture one of two things when they imagine life after corporate. Both are available if you choose them. Most people choose neither.

Myth one
It is corporate, but lighter.

Same kind of work, fewer meetings, better hours. A softer version of what you already know.

Myth two
It is an all-in entrepreneurial grind.

Raising money, building a company, working 80 hours to escape working 50.

What actually happens is stranger, and better, than either one. Nobody describes it accurately, which is exactly why it feels so scary.
  • The calendar goes blankNobody sets your priorities now. That is freedom, eventually. At first it is disorienting.
  • Income gets lumpyNo more Friday deposit. The total can be fine while the rhythm still messes with your head.
  • You lose the scoreboardNo reviews, no promotion cycle. You build your own measure of how you are doing.
  • You lose the coworkersThe one nobody warns you about. You did not just lose a job, you lost your daily social life.
  • Days stop having shapesSunday night dread disappears. So does the structure that made rest feel earned.
  • Guilt shows upResting on a Wednesday feels illegal for about a year. Busy has meant worth for three decades.

None of that means you made a mistake. That is the process, and almost everyone hits most of it. The good news is the first stretch is the hardest, you need less money than you think, and nobody is judging you. That feeling is corporate too.

The program

Design it. Fund it. Live it.

Getting out is one step. What nobody prepares you for is everything after. Thirty years of someone else setting your priorities, and then the calendar is blank. There is a sequence to this, and it is not complicated.

ONE
Design Your Life
Before you price it, decide what you actually want. Four areas worth your hours:
  • Connection
  • Mind & Body
  • Purpose
  • Fun
TWO
Fund Your Life
Find out what your life actually costs, then fund it in the fewest hours possible. There are hundreds of ways to make money that are not another full-time job. The goal is not replacing your old salary. It is funding the life you just designed.
THREE
Live Your Life
Owning all of your time is a blessing and a curse until you learn to manage it. This is the structure, the routines, and the rhythm that make the design real. Once you get it, you will never give your time away again.

Everyone is smart enough to figure this out on their own. I did. It just took me years, I did it alone, and it did not have to be that hard.

Why the community

The hardest part is not the money. It is how alone it feels.

You do not just lose a paycheck when corporate ends. You lose the coworkers, the routine, and the built-in answer to what do you do. Your friends still inside cannot relate. Your spouse is worried about the money. Nobody around you is going through the same thing at the same time.

I did it alone for years. Every mistake was one I had to make myself to learn from. Starting this community changed that, not because I finally had the answers, but because I found people asking the same questions.

The connection is the cake. The program is the icing.

  • Your circle of fiveA small group of Gen Xers on the same path, so you are never working through this by yourself.

  • Connection and collaborationNot a support group and not a lecture. People who work, ideate, and push each other forward.

Who this is for

You paid your dues. You did everything right.

It does not matter where you are standing right now. The work is the same, only the urgency changes.

Still in corporate

You have the biggest advantage there is: time and income to prepare. Starting now is not admitting anything. It is the smartest thing you can do.

Just got the news

Already out
